Contractual & Legal Analysis

The Contractual Reality (The Ensuing Loss Carve-Out)

Liberty Mutual is violating its own policy language by denying a covered ensuing loss.

Section I, Exclusion 2.c explicitly excludes the cost of repairing or replacing faulty workmanship itself (the insulation install).
However, the opening sentence of Section I, Exclusion 2 explicitly carves back full coverage:
“However, any ensuing loss to property described in Coverages A and B not excluded or excepted in this policy is covered.”
Legal Fact: Because previously sound structural components (drywall, framing, sheathing) suffered physical water damage as a consequence of the faulty install, it is a textbook covered “ensuing loss” under Maryland law.
